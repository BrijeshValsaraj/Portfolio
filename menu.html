About me

I've always believed that curiosity isn't a trait - it's a pulse. Something you feel in your fingertips when you touch a microscope slide or in your chest when an idea refuses to leave you alone. I've spent most of my life chasing questions - not just in labs, but in people, in art and in silence.

Science, for me, is less about answers and more about pursuit. I work in life sciences because I believe in stories written in cells and proteins - silent, intricate narratives that shape health, identity, and existence itself. Every experiment is a conversation with the unknown. Some days it answers back. Most days it doesn't. But I keep showing up.

But I'm not all lab coat and latex gloves. I'm also a photographer when light hits just right, a writer when insomnia wins, a guitarist when emotions scream, and a painter when they sulk. My hobbies are equal parts passion and beautifully managed chaos - like a Renaissance fair accidentally crashing a TED Talk.

If you're here for credentials - you'll find them. If you're here by accident, welcome to the rabbit hole. And if you're here to understand the person behind the pipette and paintbrush - pull up a metaphorical chair.
